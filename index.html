Prescribed Debt Removal

Prescribed debt removal is a process of writing off debts that were last paid over 3 years ago and never made acknowledgement of the debt.

Legal Framework

The prescription of consumer debts is regulated by the National Credit Act 34 of 2005 (when the credit agreement falls under the NCA) and the Prescription Act 68 of 1969. The Prescription Act generally regulates all aspects of the prescription, which would also include consumer debts, while section 126B of the National Credit Act regulates and prohibits certain practices related to prescription, such as the selling of prescribed consumer debts or the continued collection or re-activation of prescribed consumer debts.

In general, if a debtor in an applicable time period makes no payment towards settling a debt, does not acknowledge owing the debt or agree to pay it; or if the creditor does not demand payment from a debtor, start legal action against him or communicate with him in any manner and within the applicable time period, a debt becomes prescribed. This entails in essence that the debt (including a subsidiary debt which arose from such a principal debt, such as interest or a surety) is extinguished and the creditor forever loses its right to claim payment based on the debt.

Section 10(2) of the Prescription Act; Saner Prescription in South African Law para 3.3.1 at 3-36; and Jans v Nedcor Bank Ltd 2003 6 SA 646 (SCA).

Debt Review Removal

Debt review removal is the legal process to assist previously registered clients who are no longer over-indebted and can manage to maintain their debt repayments.

Understanding Debt Review Removal

Debt review removal is the legal process to assist previously registered clients who are no longer over-indebted and can manage to maintain their debt repayments. To terminate the debt review process and have the debt review notice removed from your credit reports requires that a court application be made for the court to find that you are not over-indebted, even if there is no court order in place for your debt review if your financial circumstances have improved to the point that you are now able to meet the repayments due to all your creditors, then the debt review process can be terminated.

Since you are not over-indebted anymore, and you are now able to meet the repayments due to all your creditors directly and don't need the help from a debt counsellor or debt review anymore. To complete the removal, you will obtain a court order to rescind the debt review order. Credit bureaus will update via the NCR Debt Help system about the changes in your situation and you will obtain a court order declaring that are no longer over-indebted.
